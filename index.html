Welcome

Thank you for participating in this research study from the European University Institute. You are about to read a short article and answer questions about your views on climate change.

What you will do: Read a short news-style article (~2 minutes) and complete a survey about your attitudes and views (~6 minutes). The study takes about 8 minutes in total.

Compensation: You will be paid via Prolific upon completion of the survey.

Eligibility: This study is open to adults aged 18 or older residing in the United States.

Voluntary: Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may skip any individual question you do not wish to answer, and you may withdraw at any time by closing the browser, with no consequence to your Prolific account.

Privacy: Your responses are anonymous. No names, email addresses, or IP addresses are collected. Demographic information (age, gender, education, ethnicity, political identity) is provided directly by Prolific. Please see the full Privacy Statement below before consenting.

Funding and ethics: This research is funded by the Magnus Bergvall Foundation and has been reviewed and approved by the European University Institute Ethics Committee. For questions about research ethics, contact ethics@eui.eu.

A note on the study's aims: To preserve the scientific validity of the research, we are not able to share the full purpose of this study before you begin — knowing the specific hypotheses in advance can influence how people respond. You will receive a complete debrief at the end of the survey explaining what the study is testing.

Wired to Fail? The Effect of Climate Change Narratives on Pro-Environment Attitudes and Behavior

Data Controller: Filip Olsson, European University Institute (filip.olsson@eui.eu)

What we collect: Survey responses (climate attitudes, behavioral intentions, policy preferences) and a Prolific participant ID used solely for payment verification. Demographic data (age, gender, education, ethnicity, political identity) is obtained from Prolific. No names, email addresses, or IP addresses are collected.

How we store it: Responses are collected via Qualtrics (EU-based GDPR-compliant servers) and exported to EUI institutional storage (OneDrive) accessible only to the research team.

Retention: Prolific IDs are deleted after 30 days, at which point the dataset is fully anonymised. The anonymised dataset is retained for 10 years per EUI policy.

Your rights: Under Article 16 of the EUI Data Protection Policy you have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of your data within the 30-day window. After anonymisation, individual responses can no longer be located. Contact: filip.olsson@eui.eu (DPO: data_protection_officer@eui.eu).

Ethics: This study has been approved by the EUI Ethics Committee (ethics@eui.eu).

Debrief — Thank You

Thank you for completing this survey. Your responses have been recorded.

Purpose of the study: This research examines how different framings of climate change in the media influence people's attitudes, behavioural intentions, and policy preferences. The article you read was one of several constructed narratives that participants were randomly assigned to.

Withdrawing your data: You may withdraw your responses within 30 days by emailing filip.olsson@eui.eu with your Prolific ID. After 30 days the dataset is fully anonymised and individual responses can no longer be located.
